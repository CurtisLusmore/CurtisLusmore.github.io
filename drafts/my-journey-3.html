Having spent my teenage years making primitive games, building IRC bots, and programming my calculator, it was time to move on to university. I decided to continue my programming studies, along with my other favourite subjects—mathematics and graphics (working with CAD software)—in a Bachelor of Engineering majoring in something called Infomechatronics, which I believe is a mashup of the words “information”, “mechanics” and “electronics”. I lasted all of one semester before switching out.

It was the physics course that did it. I never took physics in high school, and I just couldn’t get into it, particularly in contrast to the engineering mathematics course. In mathematics, we learnt how to derive things from first principles, we built up results from existing results and simple assumptions, we learnt how to explore the space of all mathematical ideas. The physics course, on the other hand, was more about memorising formulae that others had already discovered. So I switched to a Bachelor of Mathematics.
