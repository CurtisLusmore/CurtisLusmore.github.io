Recalibrating Success

Curtis Lusmore
Not yet published

Expressions like “Fail Fast, Fail Often” and “Embrace Failure” have always rubbed me the wrong way. Although there is real wisdom behind these expressions, there's also some nuance. The problem with expressions like these is that they tend to spread much more broadly than the real lessons behind them, and so a lot of people miss the nuance and learn the wrong lessons.

And so you often end up with people thinking “let’s just try this and see what happens, it’s okay if we fail.” And before you say “failure is a learning opportunity, what’s the problem?”
